Not in size or weight, but in its vast number of neural cells and connections. In other words, Albert Einstein had a dense brain.

How can this help you? You might be surprised.
Einstein lived to an old age — ultimately passing away from a stomach aneurysm caused by excessive pipe smoking — but even in his later years, his cognitive abilities remained highly effective.

That’s because his neural cells and connections were so numerous and dense that the kind of brain decline we associate with dementia never managed to take hold and cause damage. And so Einstein maintained his remarkable mental capacity until the end.

According to Dr. Sanjay Gupta, one of the leading experts in brain health and a researcher of new approaches for cognitive support…

“Much of memory loss is associated with the weakening of neural connections and the buildup of environmental toxins that interfere with communication between brain cells.”

But the good news, Dr. Gupta says, is that there is now a promising line of research pointing to natural ways to support both the creation of new connections and the removal of these harmful toxins.

One such discovery involves an ancient blend of Himalayan cedar honey combined with specific plant extracts, traditionally used to help cleanse the body and support brain function.

This combination — recently studied by teams led by Dr. Paul Cox — appears to help the body neutralize elements that affect mental clarity while also supporting the chemical signaling the brain uses to access memories.

In a special video presentation, Dr. Gupta describes eight simple things you can do daily to help strengthen your brain — including this natural blend that has caught the attention of the scientific community for its potential restorative effect.

He also shares a compelling case study about one of his patients who was experiencing “severe cognitive decline” and began adopting some of these strategies, later observing significant changes over time — although, of course, results may vary.
